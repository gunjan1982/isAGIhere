# AI Industry: People to Track, Sources to Monitor & Building Your Single Feed

## Overview

The AI industry moves faster than almost any other sector, with major model releases, research breakthroughs, and tooling shifts happening weekly. Building a reliable signal-to-noise system requires tracking the right mix of people — the founders who built the engine, the current operators racing the car, and the builders shipping tools — alongside the right publications across the right platforms. This report maps the essential people across LLMs and vibe coding, the best content sources, and practical tools to aggregate everything into one feed for a water-cooler-style AI community.

***

## Part 1: The Founding Generation ("Godfathers")

These are the people whose decades of research *created* the current AI wave. They all shared the 2018 Turing Award and were the subject of a 2025 FT Future of AI Summit panel titled "The Minds of Modern AI". Think of them as the people who **built the engine**.[^1][^2]

| Person | What They Founded | Social Media Presence | Stance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Geoffrey Hinton** | Backpropagation & deep neural networks; co-created AlexNet (181K+ citations)[^3] | X + LinkedIn — active post-Google departure | Existential risk alarm; left Google in 2023 to speak freely[^3] |
| **Yann LeCun** | Convolutional neural networks (CNNs); backbone of all computer vision | Very active on X (@ylecun, 972K followers)[^4] | Contrarian — argues current LLMs are fundamentally limited; combative debater[^3] |
| **Yoshua Bengio** | Solved the vanishing gradient problem; founded MILA, world's top AI research institute[^1] | LinkedIn-primary; long essays on safety | Safety-focused; less social noise, more depth[^5] |
| **Fei-Fei Li** | Created ImageNet — the dataset that triggered the deep learning explosion | X + LinkedIn; runs Stanford HAI | Human-centered AI; diversity and responsible deployment[^6] |
| **Andrew Ng** | Co-founded Google Brain; built Baidu's AI division; created Coursera + DeepLearning.AI[^7] | Very active on X (@AndrewYNg) + LinkedIn; weekly *The Batch* newsletter | Optimistic pragmatist; best bridge between research and practice[^8] |

> **The Hinton vs. LeCun debate** is one of the most intellectually interesting threads in AI right now. Hinton believes AI poses existential risks; LeCun thinks that's overblown and that LLMs are architecturally limited. Following both gives you the full spectrum of serious opinion.[^3]

## Part 7: Building Your Single Source of Truth

For a water-cooler AI community, the goal is a unified feed that surfaces signal without overwhelming. Here is the recommended stack:

### Tool Architecture

| Layer | Tool | Purpose | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **RSS/Feed Aggregator** | **Feedly** or **Inoreader** | Pull blogs, YouTube channels, newsletters, Reddit into one reader | Feedly free (100 feeds); Pro $8/mo[^39]. Inoreader free (150 feeds); Pro ~$10/mo[^39] |
| **AI News Aggregator** | **Techmeme** + **AINewsGuru** | Real-time curated tech/AI headlines | Free[^32][^33] |
| **X Monitoring** | **X Lists** (free) | Create a curated list of all handles above | Free |
| **Newsletter Hub** | **Meco** or **Inbox** | Route all newsletter subscriptions out of email into a reader | Free tier available |
| **Community Layer** | **Discord** or **Slack** | Share daily picks with your community, organized by channel (#llm-news, #vibe-coding, #research-papers) | Free |
| **Alert Layer** | **Google Alerts** | Keyword alerts for terms like "GPT-6," "Claude 4," "new LLM release" | Free[^40] |

### Recommended Feedly/Inoreader Folder Structure

1. **🔬 Research** — OpenAI Blog, DeepMind Blog, Anthropic, BAIR, The Gradient, Lilian Weng
2. **🛠️ Engineering** — Hugging Face Blog, Simon Willison, Latent Space, Alpha Signal, Chip Huyen
3. **📰 News** — Techmeme, VentureBeat AI, AINewsGuru, The Information
4. **📧 Newsletters** — The Rundown AI, TLDR AI, The Batch, Ben's Bites, Import AI
5. **🎙️ Podcasts** — Lex Fridman, Dwarkesh, Latent Space, No Priors, Hard Fork
6. **🎯 Vibe Coding** — YouTube: Matt Wolfe, Greg Isenberg, AI Foundations, Alex Finn

### X List: The Core 15 Handles

Create a private X list called "AI Signal" with these accounts for a daily scan:

`@karpathy` `@sama` `@ylecun` `@demishassabis` `@AndrewYNg` `@swyx` `@gregisenberg` `@jeffdean` `@GaryMarcus` `@AravindSrinivas` `@nateliason` `@steipete` `@simonw` `@ylecun` `@dwarkesh_sp`

### Community Curation Workflow (Water-Cooler Model)

A practical daily rhythm for feeding a community:

1. **Morning (15 min)** — Scan Feedly "News" folder + X list. Flag 3-5 items worth sharing
2. **Midday** — Drop flagged items into the community Discord/Slack with a one-line "why it matters" context
3. **Weekly digest** — Compile the week's top 5 stories using TLDR AI + The Batch as a cross-reference. Post as a weekly thread
4. **Monthly deep-dive** — Pick one long-form piece (Lilian Weng, Dwarkesh interview, Latent Space episode) as the community read/listen of the month

### Content Channel Structure for Your Community

| Channel | What Goes There |
|---|---|
| `#breaking-news` | Same-day model releases, funding rounds, major announcements |
| `#llm-research` | Paper summaries, research blog posts, technical deep dives |
| `#vibe-coding` | Tool updates, tutorials, project showcases (Cursor, Claude Code, Replit, Lovable) |
| `#weekly-digest` | Curated weekly roundup post |
| `#water-cooler` | Hot takes, debates, commentary, memes — the social glue |
| `#tools-and-products` | New AI product launches, prompt tricks, workflow tips |
| `#interviews-pods` | Clips and timestamps from Lex, Dwarkesh, Latent Space worth discussing |
